# NaijaMart E-Commerce Sales & Customer Analytics Using SQL

## Introduction

Data-driven decision-making plays a critical role in helping e-commerce businesses understand customer behavior, optimize operations, and drive revenue growth.

In this project, I analyzed transactional data from NaijaMart, a fictional Nigerian e-commerce company, using SQL. The objective was to uncover insights into customer behavior, revenue drivers, product performance, payment preferences, and delivery operations.

---

## Business Problem

NaijaMart needed visibility into:

* Customer purchasing behavior
* Revenue drivers
* Product performance
* Regional sales trends
* Payment preferences
* Delivery performance

Management required actionable insights to improve profitability, customer retention, and operational efficiency.

---

## Dataset Overview

The analysis was conducted on a relational database consisting of:

* 1,000 Customers
* 100 Sellers
* 300 Products
* 10,000 Orders
* 10,000 Deliveries
* Customer Reviews

The database contained seven interconnected tables:

* Customers
* Sellers
* Products
* Orders
* Order Details
* Deliveries
* Customer Reviews

---

## Tools Used

* MySQL
* SQL
* MySQL Workbench

---

## Business Questions

The analysis aimed to answer the following questions:

1. Which customer segments generate the most revenue?
2. Which states contribute the highest revenue?
3. Which product categories perform best?
4. What payment methods do customers prefer?
5. How efficient are delivery operations?
6. What factors influence customer satisfaction?

---

## Key Findings

### Customer Segment Analysis

Regular customers generated approximately ₦5.5 billion in revenue, making them the largest revenue-contributing segment.

### Geographic Analysis

Anambra generated the highest revenue at approximately ₦1.06 billion, followed by Enugu and Kano.

### Product Category Analysis

Computing was the highest-performing category, generating approximately ₦1.7 billion in revenue.

### Payment Analysis

USSD was the most frequently used payment method, followed closely by Debit Card and Bank Transfer.

### Delivery Performance

The average delivery time was 7.47 days, with over 70% of orders successfully delivered.

### Customer Experience

The average product rating was 3.07 out of 5, indicating opportunities to improve customer satisfaction.

---

## Recommendations

### Strengthen Customer Retention

Implement loyalty programs and targeted promotions to increase customer retention and encourage progression into higher-value customer segments.

### Expand High-Performing Categories

Increase inventory and marketing investment within the Computing category to capitalize on strong customer demand.

### Improve Delivery Operations

Reduce delivery times and address the causes of returned and failed deliveries to improve customer experience.

### Invest in High-Performing Regions

Expand marketing efforts in Anambra, Enugu, and Kano, which demonstrated strong revenue performance.

### Enhance Customer Satisfaction

Improve packaging quality, delivery reliability, and product quality assurance to increase customer ratings and repeat purchases.

---

## Conclusion

This project demonstrates how SQL can be used to transform raw transactional data into actionable business insights.

Through customer, revenue, product, payment, and operational analysis, the project identified key opportunities to improve business performance and support data-driven decision-making.
